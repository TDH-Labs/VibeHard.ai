The part nobody tells you
Most AI app builders will happily ship you an app that leaks.

They optimize for “wow, it works” — not for “my client list won't end up on the internet.” The demo dazzles. The app goes live in one click. And nobody, at any point, checked whether one customer can read another customer's records — until someone does it for real.

That's not a hypothetical. Data exposure in AI-generated apps is the category's quiet, recurring scandal — because the tools are built to say yes, and no one is paid to say wait.

How it's made

Seven gates stand between the AI and the internet.

This is the machine under the paint. Every app VibeHard writes is stopped at the door and inspected — the same checks a security engineer would run, in the same order, without exception. Not a badge. Not a promise. A process you can watch run, gate by gate, on every single build.

01gate

Static analysis

Before anything runs, the code itself is read — mechanically, line by line — for the classic ways software betrays its users.

Hunts: injection flaws, unsafe patterns, the mistakes that turn a contact form into an open door.

internal gate id · sast
02gate

Secret scan

No password, API key, or credential is allowed to be baked into the app's code — where anyone who looks could find it.

Hunts: hardcoded keys and tokens — one of the most common, most damaging mistakes in AI-generated code.

internal gate id · secrets
03gate

Supply chain

Your app stands on other people's code — libraries, packages, dependencies. Every one of them is checked against the public record of known vulnerabilities.

Hunts: known-vulnerable dependencies, before they're welded into your app.

internal gate id · depvuln
04gate

Data isolation

The one that matters most. Every table in your app's database must prove that each client can see only their own rows — enforced by the database itself, not politely requested by the app. And here's the part we're proud of: after your app goes live, we probe the running database from the outside, like an attacker would, to prove the isolation held in the real world — not just on paper.

Hunts: cross-customer data leaks — the failure that ends client relationships and practices.

internal gate ids · rls · rls-enforce · live probe
05gate

Sensitive-data handling

Apps that touch personal information get held to a higher bar: how data is collected, where it flows, what gets logged, what never should be.

Hunts: personal data in logs, careless collection, the patterns that turn a small app into a big liability.

internal gate ids · compliance · pii
06gate

Production readiness

The difference between a demo and a product: pinned dependencies, strict types, clean containers, the unglamorous discipline that keeps software standing at 3 a.m.

Hunts: demo-grade shortcuts that work today and fall over the day you depend on them.

internal gate id · prod-readiness
07gate

It actually runs

A fresh machine installs your app from nothing, builds it, boots it, and probes it — repeatedly. One green run proves nothing; every run must come up healthy.

Hunts: “works on my machine” — the oldest lie in software.

internal gate id · verify

And then the deploy itself refuses to trust us.

Passing the gates produces a cryptographically signed certificate — and the deployment machinery will not run without it. Not for us, not for a clever workaround, not for anyone. Even if every human at VibeHard wanted to ship you an unchecked app, the system is built so we can't. That's what “safe to ship” means here: not a value we hold, a property of the machine.

What you read instead

Every finding is translated into a plain-language explanation of what it means for you:

“Your app was stopped before going live: as written, one client could have seen another client's invoices. We're fixing the database rules and re-running every check. Nothing shipped.”

Most issues are fixed automatically and re-checked from gate one. The point is what never happens: an app with a known problem quietly going live because the tool wanted to feel magical. Held is a feature.

Our one weird rule

No badges. Receipts.

You will never see us claim we're “HIPAA-compliant”, “bank-grade”, or “100% secure.” Nobody honest can put those words in a hero banner — compliance belongs to your practices as much as any tool's, and “100% secure” has never once been true in the history of software.

What we do instead is stranger and better: we show you exactly what we checked. Every gate, by name. Every finding, in plain language. Every deploy, signed. If a check didn't run, the app didn't ship — and when you're the one holding other people's data, that's worth more than any badge ever printed.
