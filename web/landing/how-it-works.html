  HOW IT WORKS

One sentence in. A checked sequence out.

Nothing about this is a black box. Here's the exact path your idea takes — from the words you type to a build with a signed sentinel on it — and what stops it at every step along the way.

01 / THE SEQUENCE

Three stages. No step skipped.

01 // INTAKE

You describe it

One plain sentence, the way you'd say it to a colleague — "a portal where each client sees only their own files." A short interview fills in what the sentence implied but didn't say: who uses it, what data it holds, what's sensitive. The answers get written into a binding spec — the full breakdown is here.

02 // BUILD + GATE

It gets written, then attacked

The code gets generated against that spec. Then eleven independent gates run in fixed order — static analysis, secret scanning, a live two-tenant attack against the real database, a boot test repeated until it stops being flaky. Nothing ships on "looks right." It ships on "survived." See every gate.

03 // JUDGMENT

The rest goes to a person

Most findings get fixed and re-checked automatically — that loop is below. Findings that need judgment, not a mechanical correction, route to a vetted reviewer scoped to the exact flagged slice, who signs off on the piece a gate can't decide alone.

02 / THE REPAIR LOOP

Most builds never reach a human.

A blocked gate becomes a work order: the system rewrites the flagged piece, re-runs the gates, and repeats, bounded, until it converges. You describe what you want. The output gets debugged without you.

What it can fix alone

A missing access policy, an unpinned dependency, a leaked-into-logs field — these have a correct answer a machine can apply and a gate can verify. The fixer never gets to lower the bar to pass — weakening a check to dodge a finding is caught and the round is voided.

What goes to a person instead

If the loop exhausts its bounded rounds, the build doesn't ship broken and it doesn't wait forever either — it's held, packaged with every verdict and finding, and routed to a reviewer. That's the one exit that isn't automatic, and it's the only one that needs to be.

03 / WHAT COMES OUT THE OTHER SIDE

Not a preview. A running app, in your account.

Live at its own URL

Deployed the moment the sentinel is signed — reachable at a real URL, ready for the people you built it for.

A database that's yours

Your data lives in your own project, under your control. We build the schema and the access rules; we don't hold the keys to it after.

The same gates on every change

A later change to the app runs the identical eleven-gate line again. The checks don't stop mattering after launch day.
