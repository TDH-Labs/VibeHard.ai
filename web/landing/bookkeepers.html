  FOR BOOKKEEPERS & FIRMS

Your clients hand you their books. Your software should wall them off.

Client portals, invoicing, statement tracking — describe what your practice needs and it gets built with every client's financials isolated per account, then attacked to prove one client's numbers can't reach another's screen.

One client seeing another client's balance sheet is the kind of story that ends a bookkeeping practice. Most AI app builders optimize for how fast the portal loads. Whether the table underneath separates one client's rows from the next is left to you to notice.

HOW YOUR PRACTICE'S APP GETS BUILT

Describe it. It's checked. Each client's books stay theirs.

01

You describe the practice tool

"A portal where each client logs in and sees only their own invoices and statements." The intake interview asks what makes this sensitive and classifies financial records automatically.

02

Isolation is checked, then attacked

Every table holding financial records must carry a real access policy — Gate 4 requires it exists. Gate 6 then stands up a live database, seeds two separate clients, and tries to read one client's numbers as the other. A single leaked row blocks the build.

03

What a machine can't judge goes to a person

Financial data being sensitive also triggers a check on where it can leak besides the database. Logs and URLs are a common path, and personal data in a log line is a hard block. Anything a gate can't resolve mechanically routes to a vetted reviewer.

WHAT THIS ACTUALLY MEANS FOR CLIENT FINANCES

Proven, not promised.

Isolation gets attacked

The gate that matters here doesn't read your access policy and take it on faith — it stands up a real database, seeds two clients, and tries to break in as each one. See the full mechanism on the trust page.

Financial records don't leak through the side door

A field like an account balance or an invoice total showing up in a server log or a URL query string is exactly the kind of quiet leak that never shows up in a demo. The gate scans for the actual property access, catching it even when the field name alone wouldn't.

No badge, no certification claim

We won't tell you this makes your practice "SOC 2 certified" or any other status we don't actually hold — that's exactly the kind of fake badge this whole approach exists to avoid.

Isolated by construction

Client financial records live in a database dedicated to your practice's app alone — never a shared table commingled with any other customer's data. VibeHard provisions and manages it by default, so there's no separate account for you to set up.
